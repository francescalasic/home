Introduction

A Shift in How I Design

Reflecting on my second year in Industrial Design and comparing it to my first year I recognize a major shift in the way I view design itself and the role of a professional in this discipline. My first year mainly focused on visual communication, aesthetics and intuitive ideation. During the second year I started approaching design in a clear, structured, critical and multidisciplinary way. I gained a new awareness of how design serves to connect technology, sustainability, interaction, business, users and society itself. I consider this evolution very valuable as it served to strengthen my interest in emotionally engaging products and sustainability-driven interaction design.

This portfolio reflects my growth between year one and year two by showing how my projects, reflections, collaborations and future goals shaped the development of my professional identity.

Project Overview

Year Two Projects

Several year two projects shaped my development.

Biomaterials & Sustainability

Terravy & Reivy

Terravy and Reivy focused on transforming ivy green waste into a bio-based material and into a biodegradable plant pot and DIY system. This project strengthened my interest in sustainability, biomaterials, material experimentation and circular value creation.

Speculative Design

Speculative Dinner

My Speculative Dinner project explored how ambiguity, discomfort and designed artifacts can influence social behavior. It helped me gain insights about facilitation, participation and the ethical responsibility of designing experiences.

Interaction & Narrative

Cluebound

Cluebound was a mystery exploration game where I contributed to visual storytelling, atmosphere and the overall game experience. It helped me connect aesthetics, narrative, user engagement and interaction design.

Prototyping

Exploratory Making

Exploratory Making supported my growth in physical prototyping, mechanisms, materials and iterative making.

About Me

Professional Identity

As a designer , I identify as someone capable of combining creativity, emotional sensitivity and experimentation. My interest often lies in projects involving storytelling,material exploration, interaction and atmosphere creation. I enjoy developing concepts able to create emotional engagement and meaningful experiences rather than purely functional interactions. Year two served to highlight how my strongest contributions often involve ideation, visual communication, concept thinking and creating coherent narratives and aesthetics around an idea.

When entering in a professional environment a major strength I recognize in myself is adaptability. Collaborative projects served me to learn the importance of adapting my personal beliefs to the different stages of the design process and to the perspective of others. My first year had me taking roles within the teams I was part of that were in my comfort zone. I would mainly focus on visual tasks and aesthetics. However during this past year I was pushed toward research, ideation, testing and experimentation. My curiosity motivated me to explore unfamiliar methods and tools such as biomaterials, electronics workshops and speculative iteration.

Another strength I recognize is my reflective attitude. When designing for a project I continuously question outcomes, assumptions and design decisions so that it's easier to identify areas of improvement. When dealing with user experiences and social interactions I pay close attention to approach these situations with empathy and emotional awareness.

Compared to year one, I now see clearly my role as a designer. I no longer reduce design to visual creation, but I now see it as a process combining research, experimentation, reflection, communication, interaction and responsibility.

Areas to develop

On the other hand this second year made me aware of my weaknesses. I tend to get attached to conceptual depth. This often makes my ideas overly ambitious or difficult to simplify. Often I explore too many possibilities, resulting in lack of efficiency and feasibility when it comes to narrowing down decisions. Another gap that i recognize happens during technical discussions: I tend to hesitate when I feel a lack of confidence in my technical knowledge.

These insights were particularly valuable to shape my PDP goals as they highlighted my need for improvement in areas like: technical independence, simplify concepts earlier and better balance experimentation with feasibility.

Design Vision

Design as Relationship

Design for me doesn't stop at the creation of products or services but it also regards the creation of relationships between people, materials, systems, emotions and environments. I believe that design shouldn't stop at solving practical problems, but it should also encourage reflection, interaction and awareness. My design vision focuses strongly on emotional engagement, sustainability and participatory experiences.

Emotional Engagement

For me users aren't passive receivers of solutions but active participants within a system or an experience. This became clear to me during Speculative Dinner, where ambiguity and discomfort encouraged participants to collectively interpret the situation offered. This project showed me how design can create meaning not only through clarity but also through tension, uncertainty and discussion.

Sustainability

Sustainability is another key aspect of my vision. For me sustainable design isn't only defined as reducing environmental impact, but also creating stronger relationships between people and materials while encouraging awareness of ecological systems. My project Terravy explored how green waste can become both a biodegradable product and a participatory experience. This strengthened my view that sustainability isn't only a technical discipline, but also emotional, educational and participatory.

Technology as Medium

Another important role in my vision is covered by Technology. My definition of technology sees it not as an isolated tool, but as a means to communication, storytelling and interaction. My project Study Buddy proved this definition as true, as I was able to use physical computing and emotional expressions in a combination to influence study behaviors and create a sense of companionship. This also proved that technology becomes meaningful when it supports human experiences rather than controlling them.

Participatory Experience

To refine my design vision and fill the gaps in my development I will continue working on projects involving biomaterials, computing, participatory interaction and speculative design. I will also make use of internships, independent prototyping and material experimentation to create design experiences that are emotionally meaningful, environmentally conscious and socially reflective.

Speculative Thinking

Design for me doesn't stop at solving practical problems but should also encourage reflection, interaction and awareness. My vision focuses on participatory experiences where people actively shape meaning together.

Systems Awareness

Design decisions are connected to broader systems like consumption, waste, social values and ecological responsibility. Material experimentation, user participation and circular value all influence each other in ways that shape the final outcome.

Reflection

Collaboration

During this last year my collaboration skills improved significantly. I gained confidence when adapting my ideas to group decisions and defined my contributions beyond simple visual tasks. The project Terravy taught me to let go of personal attachment to certain ideas when another direction better supports the collective goal. In speculative dinner we went further with collaboration, extending it to the participants themselves. This proved how designers continuously collaborate with users and stakeholders.

Compared to year one, my collaboration skills improved significantly during year two. I became more comfortable adapting my ideas to group decisions and contributing beyond visual tasks. During Terravy, I learned to let go of personal attachment to ideas when another direction supported the collective goal better. In Speculative Dinner, collaboration extended beyond teammates to the participants themselves, showing me how designers continuously collaborate with users and stakeholders. I still want to improve my confidence in technical discussions and become more proactive in coordinating group processes.

Reflection

Organising and Planning

This second year was very important for me to learn to structure the organization of activities within projects. Having a clear structure gave me the opportunity to better plan my goals and divide projects into smaller stages.

I was able to implement planning tools and shared documentation to improve coordination within group projects.

Although my planning improved, I still recognize the need to do better by becoming faster in narrowing down ideas and validating feasibility earlier.

Reflection

Critical Attitude

Year two strengthened my ability to critically reflect on assumptions, methods and design decisions. Through the way my speculative dinner was structured and all the trial and error during Terravy, I learned to see uncertainty and failed experiments as valuable segments of the design process.

Reflection gave me a new awareness of the ethical and social contexts that surround us. I started noticing with more attention how design has the ability to influence behavior and participation.

This reflective attitude gives me the tools to balance emotional depth and conceptual richness with clarity and feasibility.

Expertise Areas

Integration of Expertise

Business and Entrepreneurship

It became clear to me how important Business and Entrepreneurship is to understand how design creates value within larger systems. In my project Terravy I referred to value proposition canvases, personas and PESTEL analysis to explore how sustainability could become both meaningful and feasible. This highlighted how sustainable design isn't only about environmental impact but also about creating value for users and stakeholders. Business and entrepreneurship is a main focus in design as strong concepts require strategic thinking and realistic implementation in order to create long term impact.

Creativity and Aesthetics

Creativity and aesthetics is the expertise areas I feel the most confident about. It directly connects to my interests in emotional engagement and storytelling in design. In Cluebound, I explored atmosphere, visual storytelling and mystery to influence user immersion. In the speculative experience of Speculative dinner aesthetics shaped the entirety of the experience, starting from the cutlery prototypes to the unclearly designed menu to the controlled environment created. Both of these projects helped me understand how this expertise areas isn't only about visuals but also about behaviors and emotional tools that shape how users experience and interpret certain interaction.

Math, Data and Computing

Math, Data and Computing is still a developing area for me, but year two helped me become more confident using structured logic and technology in design. Through Arduino workshops, sensors and interaction logic, I learned how computation can support emotional and interactive experiences. Terravy also showed me how structured analysis methods can support decision-making and organization. This reflection made me realize that computing is most valuable to me when it strengthens interaction and communication rather than functioning only technically.

Technology and Realisation

Technology and Realization became one of my biggest growth areas during year two. In Terravy, I explored biomaterials, prototyping methods and material experimentation, while Exploratory Making strengthened my physical prototyping skills. Repeated testing taught me how realization is closely connected to iteration and failure. Reflecting on these experiences helped me understand that technical realization is not separate from creativity, but necessary to transform speculative and conceptual ideas into tangible experiences.

User and Society

User and Society strongly influenced my understanding of ethics, participation and human behavior. Speculative Dinner showed how ambiguity, discomfort and authority can influence social interaction and behavior. The project Terravy showed me how sustainability deeply roots from user awareness and participation. These projects were important as they brought me to realize that design doesn't only solve problems but it can also encourage reflection, discussion and behavioral awareness.

Connected, Not Separate

These expertise areas are interconnected rather than separate. In all my projects I applied multiple expertise areas simultaneously. Terravy especially demonstrated how User and Society, Technology and Realization, and Business and Entrepreneurship overlap through material experimentation, user participation and circular value creation.

Development Plan

Personal Development Plan

1

My short-term development focuses on technical confidence, simplification and stronger project planning. By the end of the semester I'll be able to complete three small arduino based prototypes to become more confident in involving sensors and outputs. By doing this I directly address my hesitation in technical decision making and I will also strengthen my independence in prototyping.

2

A second short-term goal is to test the core interaction of future projects within the first three weeks. This will help avoid overcomplicating concepts before validating their feasibility. I also intend to use lowfi prototypes in the early stages of my projects to gather feedback from users and peers sooner.

3

A third goal is to improve how I communicate practical value. I'll achieve this I want to create at least one user journey and one value proposition canvas in the early stages of my next project, before finalizing the concept. This will add value to my own development as I will be able to connect my ideas to users, stakeholders and possible implementation possibilities more clearly.

4

Looking further ahead and at my current design vision and personal identity, I want to consolidate my position through an internship. This will be a major step to help build a portfolio able to communicate clearly my identity as a designer focused on emotional engagement, sustainability and participatory interaction.

5

By the end of my bachelor my final goal will be to be able to combine conceptual thinking and technical realization with confidence on my abilities. Realistically, I want to be able to imagine meaningful experiences while being able to prototype, test, explain and evaluate them independently.

Journey

Past, Present and Future

Past

Curiosity and Visual Thinking

My past helped shaping me into a designer that approached the discipline through curiosity and visual thinking. Before university, I reduced design to aesthetics and artistic creation. This perspective remained dominant in my year one as I relied on visual communication, form and conceptual exploration as my main focus.

Present

Reflective and Multidisciplinary

Right now I recognize in myself a radical change in the way I approach projects. I am now a more reflective and multidisciplinary designer. I gained awareness of the relationship between users, materials, technology, systems and society itself. While I still value aesthetics and emotional engagement, I now acknowledge that a strong design requires research, structure, feasibility, collaboration and critical responsibility.

Future

Sustainable Systems and Interaction

In the future I will continue to develop in the direction of sustainable systems, biomaterials and interactive products. My interest mainly falls in projects involving interactive installations, sustainable product service systems and speculative experiences involving a material driven approach. In the future I aim to not only focus on problem solving, but to create work that is able to encourage reflection, participation and more conscious relationships between humans, technology and nature.
